Department of Human Anatomy

Anatomy is the first subject taught to medical students. We aim to provide a conceptual knowledge of human anatomy delivered by interactive lectures, small group discussions, and clinical case demonstrations. Our goal is to encourage students to derive the anatomical basis of clinical conditions themselves.

Vision

To provide comprehensive fundamental knowledge of human anatomy to undergraduate and post graduate students that help them understand the normal structure and function of the human body.

Mission

To train students to recognize deviation from normal anatomy in order to diagnose and treat patients effectively in the future, integrating modern technology like CT and MRI.

Infrastructure Highlights

Histology Laboratory

Equipped for 90 students with separate stations, individual microscopes, and projection screens for demonstration.

Dissection Hall

Spacious hall with 20 tables catering to 200 students, excellent ventilation, lighting, and embalming facilities.

The Museum

State-of-the-art museum displaying 180 specimens across neuroanatomy, embryology, and gross anatomy sections.

Teaching Methodology

  • Emphasis on Systemic Anatomy and Problem-Based Learning.
  • Integrated teaching methods utilizing clinical correlation.
  • Use of modern imaging technology (CT and MRI) in education.

Teaching Philosophy

The basics of anatomy are taught to encourage students to derive anatomical bases for clinical conditions.

  • Interactive lectures & Small group discussions.
  • Clinical case demonstrations.
  • Continued integration into clinical subjects in later years.

Additional Facilities

  • Departmental library with standard textbooks.
  • 2 Demonstration rooms (100 capacity each) with AV aids.
  • Dedicated Embalming room with deep freezers.
  • Skeleton & Dissected specimens displayed in hall.
