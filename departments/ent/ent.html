Department of ENT

The Department of Otorhinolaryngology (ENT) provides comprehensive care for diseases related to the Ear, Nose, and Throat, as well as Head and Neck surgery. We are committed to excellence in patient care, audiological services, and medical education.

Vision

To be a center of excellence in ENT care, providing advanced surgical solutions such as microsurgery and endoscopic sinus surgery, while training future specialists with a strong foundation in clinical skills and ethics.

Mission

To provide high-quality diagnostic and therapeutic services to the community. To conduct research in hearing impairment and speech disorders, and to offer rehabilitation services for the speech and hearing impaired.

Infrastructure & Services

Surgical Facilities

State-of-the-art Operation Theatres equipped with operating microscopes for microsurgery and high-definition endoscopes for FESS.

Audiology & Speech

Sound-treated rooms for Pure Tone Audiometry (PTA), Impedance Audiometry, and speech therapy services for rehabilitation.

Specialty Clinics

Dedicated clinics for Vertigo, Cancer screening (Head & Neck), and Voice disorders to provide specialized care.

Clinical Procedures

  • Tonsillectomy & Adenoidectomy.
  • Septoplasty & Functional Endoscopic Sinus Surgery (FESS).
  • Tympanoplasty & Mastoidectomy.

Academic Activities

Training undergraduates and interns in diagnostic and surgical skills.

  • Temporal Bone Dissection Workshops.
  • Clinical case presentations & Journal Clubs.
  • Training in rigid and flexible endoscopy.

Facilities

  • Daily ENT OPD.
  • Inpatient Wards (Male/Female).
  • Departmental Library & Museum.
