Department of Physiology

Physiology is a branch of medicine that deals with the study of functions of the human body and the regulation of different systems. The department is dedicated to providing comprehensive knowledge of integrated organ functions, which forms the basis for understanding health and disease.

Vision

To provide the best in class training to students in basic and applied Physiology by adopting the most relevant teaching-learning methods and contributing significant new knowledge to the society through need-based quality research.

Mission

To provide comprehensive knowledge of the integrated functions of organ systems and equip students with skills to interpret experimental data, distinguishing between normal and abnormal to identify and treat diseases effectively.

Infrastructure & Facilities

Research Laboratory

A well-equipped laboratory dedicated to research purposes for faculty and students to conduct physiological studies.

CAL Facility

Computer Assisted Learning (CAL) technique facility provided for amphibian and other experimental practicals.

Departmental Library

Stocked with updated books and resources available for both undergraduates and faculties.

Activities

  • Conducting experiments to study physiological phenomena.
  • Interpretation of experimental and investigative data.
  • Research to understand body functions and disease mechanisms.

Teaching Philosophy

The curriculum focuses on the coordinated body function and the relative contribution of each organ system to the maintenance of the milieu interior.

  • Integrated teaching of organ systems.
  • Skills to distinguish normal vs abnormal data.
  • Understanding physiological adaptations to environment.

Facilities

  • Hematology and Clinical Physiology Labs.
  • Demonstration rooms with AV aids.
  • Departmental library.
  • Dedicated Research Laboratory.
