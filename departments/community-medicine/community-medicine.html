Department of Community Medicine

We are committed to preparing medical graduates to become effective and efficient Community Physicians. Our focus is on percolating the importance of "Good Health" at the grass-root level while promoting higher standards in medical education and research.

Vision

To provide comprehensive community health services and promote research relevant to local and national needs. We aim to foster life-long, self-directed learning and leadership skills in our students.

Teaching Philosophy

Utilize innovative and modern methods to maintain higher standards in education. We promote student-centered learning and keep liaison with local, national, and international institutions to exchange knowledge.

Infrastructure & Training Centers

Department Museum

An educational space featuring exhibits on the history of community medicine, public health milestones, and disease prevention strategies.

Field Stations

Strategically located facilities to support extensive fieldwork and community outreach programs, providing practical experience in diverse settings.

Research Labs

Designed for epidemiological, biostatistical, and public health research, equipping students with tools for cutting-edge investigations.

Best Practices

  • Family Adoption: Coverage of 1000+ families across RHTC and UHTC areas.
  • Screening Camps: Over 450 camps organized, including female health screening in Banaskantha.
  • Adopted Villages: Serving Kushkal, Moriya, Malana, Sangla, Akedi, and others.

Curriculum

Total Teaching Hours: 435 (Phase I-III). Crafted according to CBME guidelines.

  • Small Group Discussions (SGD) & Self-Directed Learning (SDL).
  • Field Visits & Industrial/Corporate Visits.
  • Family Adoption Programme & Project Work.

Future Plans

  • Expand family adoption to 5 more villages.
  • Foster social bonding through community service.
  • Support slow learners through remedial tutorials.
  • Encourage interdisciplinary and undergraduate research.
