Department of Pharmacology

The Department of Pharmacology is dedicated to imparting patient-focused and community-oriented knowledge regarding the therapeutic basis of essential drugs. We strive to understand the mechanism of disease and drug action to apply this knowledge in patient care.

Vision

To be a Department of Excellence by imparting knowledge, skills, and values pertaining to the therapeutic basis of essential, life-saving drugs. We focus on their rational use, safety, and suitability for clinical conditions with due attention to Pharmacovigilance.

Mission

To deliver the best education through innovative teaching methodologies and virtual lab training. We aim to identify and treat adverse drug reactions (ADR) via the PvPI program, develop newer treatment strategies, and encourage community outreach to educate the public on safe medication use.

Key Initiatives & Facilities

Pharmacovigilance (PvPI)

Active monitoring, identification, and analysis of Adverse Drug Reactions (ADR) through the established AMC of BMCRI.

Virtual Lab Training

Implementation of newer teaching methodologies including virtual labs to enhance learning abilities and critical thinking.

Community Outreach

Campaigns and street plays to educate the public on safe medicine use, self-medication risks, and reporting adverse reactions.

Objectives

  • Promote rational use of medicines and antibiotics.
  • Administer caution for drug use in pediatric, geriatric, and pregnant populations.
  • Develop ethics, skills, and attitudes to serve as a "Physician of first contact".

Academic Focus

The curriculum emphasizes self-reliance and self-education through stimulating intellectual discourse.

  • Analytical and critical thinking skill development.
  • Innovative, basic, collaborative, and translational research.
  • Faculty development programs.

Infrastructure

  • Experimental Pharmacology Labs.
  • Computer Aided Learning (CAL) Lab.
  • Departmental Library.
  • Pharmacovigilance Centre.
