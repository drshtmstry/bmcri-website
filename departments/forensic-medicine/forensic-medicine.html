Forensic Medicine & Toxicology

The Department of Forensic Medicine & Toxicology has been in existence since the inception of this institution. We believe in group dynamics and working integrated with different departments to provide comprehensive medicolegal education.

Vision

To facilitate group dynamics and work integrated with different departments of the institute while maintaining state-of-the-art facilities for legal medicine education and expert opinion.

Mission

To deliver conceptual knowledge through interactive lectures and small group discussions, giving special emphasis to ECE (Early Clinical Exposure) and SDL (Self-Directed Learning).

Infrastructure & Facilities

Mortuary Complex

Well-equipped mortuary to demonstrate Postmortem examination, featuring a 4-body cold storage facility.

Museum & Anthropology

State-of-the-art museum with wet specimens, ballistics, and anthropology instruments for research and sex/age determination.

Medicolegal Training

Practical training in Autopsy techniques, Injury Certificates, Medical Law & Ethics, and Court procedures.

Teaching Concept

Knowledge is delivered via interactive lectures, small group discussions, and clinical case presentations.

  • Emphasis on ECE and SDL.
  • Student feedback and reflections after formative assessment.
  • Role Play and Poster Presentations.

Internship Training

Interns receive focused training on practical medicolegal work.

  • Autopsy techniques & Drunkenness examination.
  • Injury Certificates, MCCD, & Rape Victim examination.
  • Age Estimation & Medical Law discussions.

Educational Assets

  • Facilities to teach 200 students per batch.
  • Library with >85 textbooks & reading room.
  • Anthropology instruments for stature studies.
  • Demonstration halls with LCD projectors.
