Department of Dentistry

Dentistry is the branch of medicine concerned with the prevention and treatment of oral diseases, including teeth, supporting structures, and soft tissues. We strive to provide patient care to the underprivileged by leveraging state-of-the-art technology.

Vision

To empower and educate undergraduate medical students regarding Dental sciences and its interconnectedness with systemic diseases. We aim to help students understand the scope of various dental treatment modalities to improve quality of life.

Mission

To provide comprehensive care for oral diseases, malformations, and anomalies. We focus on early detection of oral cancer and understanding the systemic manifestations of oral conditions while serving the community.

Scope & Specialties

Core Specialties

Includes Orthodontics, Pediatric Dentistry, Periodontics, Prosthodontics, Endodontics, and Oral & Maxillofacial Surgery.

Diagnostic Focus

Emphasis on identifying, diagnosing, and understanding the sequel of oral conditions, including early detection of Oral Cancer.

Patient Care

Treatment of oral diseases, correction of jaw malformations, misalignment of teeth, and management of birth anomalies like cleft palate.

Student Learning

During the 15-day clinical posting, students learn to evaluate overall health while advising on oral hygiene.

  • Brief clinical lectures followed by patient observation.
  • Understanding systemic manifestations of oral diseases.
  • Observation of various dental treatment modalities.

Teaching Methods

Conceptual knowledge is delivered through interactive sessions.

  • Small group teaching using Models, Charts, and Histories.
  • Clinical case demonstrations.
  • Assignments and interactive lectures.

Infrastructure

  • State-of-the-art Dental Chairs & Equipment.
  • Oral and Maxillofacial Radiology.
  • Dedicated Teaching Areas for small groups.
